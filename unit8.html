Information Technology and its Social Impacts

Information Technology (IT) is the convergence of information and communication technologies, revolutionizing global communication and information flow. IT has integrated into various sectors like education, industry, healthcare, and research, fostering advancements and connectivity worldwide.

Social Impacts of IT:

Positive Impacts

  • Enhanced Communication: Instant global communication.
  • Educational Advancements: Tools for teaching and learning, enabling distance education.
  • Industrial Efficiency: Improved product quality and quantity control.
  • Healthcare Improvements: Better patient management and treatment methodologies.
  • Entertainment and Research: Advances in animation, entertainment, and scientific research, including space technology.

Negative Impacts

  • Cybercrime: Increased risks of data theft, privacy breaches, and virus распространение.
  • Social Disruption: Potential for misuse to disturb social peace and security.

Information Security Defined

Information Security is the practice of protecting network infrastructure and data from unauthorized actions, encompassing prevention, detection, and response to threats. It involves physical and software measures to safeguard computers, users, and programs, creating a secure cyber environment.

Objectives of Information Security:

  • Confidentiality: Ensuring data access is restricted to authorized individuals only.
  • Integrity: Maintaining data accuracy and completeness, preventing unauthorized alteration.
  • Availability: Guaranteeing timely and reliable access to information for authorized users.

Cryptography for Data Security

Cryptography, meaning "secret writing," is the science of securing information through encryption and decryption. It's crucial for confidential communication across various sectors.

Encryption and Decryption

  • Encryption: Converting readable "plaintext" into unreadable "ciphertext" using algorithms and keys to protect its content during transmission or storage.
  • Decryption: The reverse process of converting ciphertext back into plaintext, requiring appropriate keys to restore readability.

Digital Divide: Access and Literacy Gap

The Digital Divide represents the disparity between individuals with and without access to Information Technology and the skills to use it. This gap exists across demographics such as urban vs. rural populations, education levels, and socioeconomic status, posing a global challenge to equitable technology access and literacy.

Computer Ethics and Cyber Ethics

Computer Ethics, also known as Cyber Ethics, are moral guidelines for computer professionals to prevent misuse of technology. They promote responsible and ethical behavior in the digital world.

Examples of Computer Ethics

  • Respect user privacy; do not use others' passwords.
  • Protect intellectual property; avoid software piracy.
  • Ensure data integrity; do not alter data without authorization.
  • Prevent harm; do not use computers to spread malware or misinformation.
  • Uphold honesty; do not misuse electronic transactions or bear false witness online.

Cyber Crime: Misuse of Technology

Cyber crime refers to illegal activities involving computers, the internet, and other digital technologies. It exploits technology for malicious purposes, causing harm to individuals and society.

Examples of Cyber Crimes

  • Data theft and unauthorized access to information.
  • Malware distribution (viruses, worms, Trojans).
  • Software piracy and copyright infringement.
  • Privacy violations and disclosure of secrets.
  • Online harassment and offensive content dissemination.
  • Financial cybercrimes (transaction misuse, salami slicing).
  • System attacks (hacking, DOS attacks).

Cyber Law: Legal Framework for Cyberspace

Cyber Law provides the legal framework governing the use of computers and the internet. It aims to enforce cyber ethics and deter cybercrimes by establishing legal boundaries and penalties for illegal online activities. In Nepal, the Electronic Transaction Ordinance, 2005, serves as the cyber law.

Provisions of Cyber Law

  • Electronic and Digital Signature Law: Legalizes digital signatures for online transactions.
  • Intellectual Property Law: Protects digital creations, including copyright, trademarks, and patents.
  • Telecommunication Law: Regulates online communication and service providers.
  • Data Protection and Privacy Law: Safeguards personal data and online privacy.

IT Policy of Nepal: Objectives

Nepal's IT Policy aims to prioritize IT development and leverage it for socio-economic progress.

Objectives of IT Policy

  • Establish IT as a high-priority sector.
  • Promote knowledge-based industries and employment.
  • Enhance e-governance for effective public service delivery.
  • Increase IT usage across sectors for productivity.
  • Facilitate access to international IT advancements.
  • Strengthen the Government Integrated Data Center (GIDC) for IT infrastructure.
